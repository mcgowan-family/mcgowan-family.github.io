Summer 2014

One of the biggest things that will happen to us this year, was Sarah selling her business, The Upper Crust. This was a really big step for all of us, but obviously mostly for Sarah. As a sign of the affection she was held in by her neighbours of many years, the guys down the road made her this lovely cake!

Life at home continues much as it has for the past year. There are parties to go to, we started into the kitchen, we ripped some old stuff out, we made some holes in the wall and it was very satisfying.

I was lucky enough to get a few days away in the Alps with Andrew.

It was a lovely Spring. But on those damp days, there were plenty of indoor options. Apparently Bryher was very good at climbing. And Patrick was great too :)

We had an absolutely lovely week in a little town called Shaldon, on the south coast of Devon.

We're right into the beekeeping now. But it doesn't always go to plan!

The birthdays kept coming. People tell me this was a significant one.

In May, I was lucky enough to join a great course run out of MIT in Boston. Among the many things I did there, one of the more memorable was spending some time, including sharing an evening meal, with Dave Clark, one of the recognised pioneers of the internet. Among his many conrtibutions, is the classic line, "We reject: kings, presidents, and voting. We believe in: rough consensus and running code.".

And in a summer of sport when Brazil crashed out of the World Cup to Germany, Murray crashed out of Wimbledon, Mark Cavendish made it three by crashing out of a fantastic start to the Tour de France 2014 in Yorkshire.

cheers, Paul
