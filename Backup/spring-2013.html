Spring 2013

One of the big changes coming up, is that The McGowans are moving home agian. We had no plans to, but a home I had always loved came on the market and, amazingly, it looks like we move in next week!!!

I'm not sure what the occasion was, but the kids love to help, when Mum gets in the kitchen.

Another year, another birthday. This time with a Star Wars theme.

Christmas is just brilliaint with the kids, and who would miss the school Nativity. I'm not entirely sure how the pig fits into the feast of the King of the Jews, but probably best not to dwell on that.

Another tradition is making the cake, and everyone wants to get involved in that.

Finally, Christmas Day arrives. This year, it was bikes all round. Then there was more cooking.

Wee Michael and Geraldine were back from New Zealand, so we all trooped up to Glasgow to see them and the rest of the clan. Glasgow Council did a great job for families in Georges Square. Patrick loved the carousel. We ended up as the events model family, which was pretty funny. With pictures in the Glasgow Herald and look out for us in the run up to next years event! Patrick and Bryher loved it all. And of course we had the compulsory trip up to Mugdock Park.

It wasn't a bad winter and we even got some decent snow. A great sledging trip was marred a bit by the kids getting way too cold. Need to get more cold weather gear next time I'm in the US.

I was really pleased to get away skiing with Andrew and Hilary mid March (12 thru 15). A real treat to spend time with friends in the mountains. Andrew and Hilary drove over and met Andrew's cousin Brian in his normal winter domicile of Champoluc. I flew out and met them later in the week.

We had a very early camping trip down to Watchet in March. Imogen and the kids came with us and we stayed in a caravan, but it was too cold really. Lesson learned for next year :)

Patrick has a great little bunch of mates in reception blue class. And the Mums and Dads get on well too. This is Billy (and wee brother Sebastien), Ollie, Jakie and Freddie. Outside the Aquarium in Bristol.

cheers, Paul
