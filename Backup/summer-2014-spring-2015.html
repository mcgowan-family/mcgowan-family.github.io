' Right Here

Summer 2014 through to Winter 2014/2015

The bee story went from strength to strength last year. This is us uncapping and then extracting the very first honey. Who knew we'd be producing our own honey in our first year at the Rectory :) We use a lot ourselves, give it to friends & family and sell it in work and down at Roots & Fruits on the Bath Road.

We had some regular company in the garden now in the form of Nelson the cat from Eve and Chris's next door. A really lovely cat. Very old though (into his 20's) and unfortunately he died later in 2014.

Every day in the garden is a treat. And the evening's are pretty great too :)

In August, we finally got to go down and stay at Thistledown near Nymphfield. Great campsite. Patrick was looking cool in some borrowed head gear. We all headed off for a walk down into the Woodchester Valley and found some great swings on the way. Further on down we came across a great little boathouse (Dave and a very pregnant Jules, Steve & Ash and the 4 of us).Bryher was looking very cute with a great big furry caterpillar! We eventually made it up to Woodchester Mansion which was amazing! Steve, Beth & Seth and Ian, Helen and Taryn were all there too. It made for a nice camp :)

In mid-August we spent a great week camping at Watermouth Bay near Ilfracombe.

Lots of of fun for the kids with their friends over the summer. I think this was Jake's birthday party at Matson. And then we went over to Highnam and the Watts joined us.

We went back to Ilfracombe in September and took Peter and Pat with us. We stayed at a fabulous little cottage on the hill over the harbour. Great spot with a lovely view of Ilfracombe. Verity was still holding dominion over the harbour. We went back to the fabulous Watermouth Bay Castle, and grandma and grandpa went for a spin. And to cap off the week, the Clyde steamer Waverley paid us a visit!

But the holidays were finally over, and it was time for everyone to go back to school. Patrick didn't initially get a place at Leckhampton, but that was great as far as he was concerned as he got to start into Year 2 with all of his old mates at Charlton Kings. Meanwhile Bryher was getting ready for the big day. Now I don't know if these things are connected, but around that time, she went for her first proper hair do. The end result was certainly Bryher, but not as we knew her! And then it was Bryher's turn to start into Reception at Leckhampton. That's better, looks like my wee girl again :)

As ever, the work on the house is never done. Painting the west side of the house felt like a great job, and Sarah got stuck in. As ever, we made it a family affair.

We finally made good on our promise to head to Peppa Pig World. These slides were great fun and the kids enjoyed them too :) There was a train ride, which always goes down well. There were some big scary rides that the boys went on. And then there was this monster that we all went on!

October came and our wee girl was growing up, but not too much. Everyone crowds round the cake to sing happy 5th birthday.

Autumn was definiitely upon us. Can't recall what was happening at school this day, but they obviously had fun. The west face of the house had looked like a building site for most of the year, but one day in Novemeber, it got to breathe again.

Autumn was turning into Winter, and still the works went on. We had started into the conservatory with a vengeance. Looking at it here, you get a sense of the scale of the task. As always, there were nice wee bits of history to discover. Then of course we had Patrick's 7th birthday, which he shared at a great Lego birthday party with Oli. Bryher's friend Eva got to come too.

Christmas was soon upon us. It was a lovely Christmas at the Rectory. We made sure we spread the Christmas cheer. And then it was baths all round and, finally, a good nights sleep. Michael, Geraldine, John-Ross and Erin came to stay in just after Christmas. Then we headed up to Glasgow in between Christmas and New Year and caught up with all the family up North. Patrick and Bryher loved catching up with all their cousins. And we all loved living in the West End for a few days.

Back in Cheltenham, and Patrick started off the New Year by getting into rugby at Old Pat's for a few weeks. He certainly seemed to enjoy it.

Just before Winter was through, we all headed off to Chamonix for our first Winter Holiday!
