Spring 2014

As we mentioned last time, we were on the move. The sale eventually went through in April 2013 and we moved in during July.

We had a very enjoyable summer. Lazy picnics in the garden, getting the family round, getting friends round and generally having fun.

We did some work as well. But it was all about finding out about the Old Rectory and who else lived here. Turned out quite a few others lived here. In all sorts of shapes and sizes.

Who needs a swimming pool! Sometimes Godliness comes after cleanliness. We organised for Patrick and his classmates to get a bit of ecumenical broadening at the Cheltenham synagogue.

Shortly after the sale, we headed off for our annual pilgrimage to St.Davids.

    As the summer wore on, the kids got feral, we all got relaxed, I had a great idea and we went from no pets to 100 000 pets.

    Everything came to a very dramatic halt though, when Patrick managed to pull one of the old massive cast iron radiators off the wall. It happened in a part of the house we hadn't started work on, but a combination of old fittings and new enthusiasm riupped it off the wall. It threw him wide as it fell, and fortunately missed everyone else, but landed on his foot as it crashed to the ground. You wouldn't want to se a picture of the foot at the time, but this was it some time after a fabulous operation by the team from Frenchay that took place at Gloucester Royal. There was so much damage to the foot that the plan had been to do a skin graft, but a decision taken in the operating theatre went for a straight suture, which was a massive result.

    There was a lot of family support for our little wounded boy. And he didn't miss out on much - including Bryher's 4th birthday party!!! And when Ashwin had a birthday party in the woods, Patrick and Bryher came along to that too. No chance of losing Bryher in this outfit!

    We popped down to @Bristol in February. Always fun. Educational too! I think Bryher's head might be back at the Rectory. Dyson could learn a thing or two. Anything you can do ...

    All things have their season, and we've come through a tough one. All of us in one room for 3 months without heating, then moving back into the front of the house. Patrick's accident. Birthday's, Christmas, starting work on the back of the house. But as the days get longer and we can see the end of the major works inside the house, it feels like it's not just the Spring flowers that are shaking off the grip of Winter.
