' Cheese Rolling

Cheese Rollers

late May Bank Holiday 2006

Doubtless you've all heard of the cheese rolling in Gloucestershire. But if you haven't taken the chance to find out why cider can damage your health, I encourage you to wander up to Coopers Hill on a May Bank Holiday and see what fermented apple juice can do to you.

Every year, towards the end of May, the few and the brave, climb to the top of Coopers Hill in Gloucestershire to chase a cheese down a hill.

As you can see, the cheese invariably wins, but that doesn't stop the girls having a go.

This years ladies race turned into a southern hemisphere finale, with an Ozzie chasing down a fiery Kiwi into the waiting arms of the local rugby team.

They all get up a fair pace, and as you can see from this high impact face plant from the South Island amazon who claimed the Double Gloucester on this day, it's not something you always walk away from.

The day started off sunny, but there were a few heavy showers. The sensible folk had all worn appropriate headgear.

Just as well I don't drink cider :o)
