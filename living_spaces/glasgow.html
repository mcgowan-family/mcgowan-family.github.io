Managed to make myself useful at Clochbar by laying some lawn. The happy customer, now wants to invest in some croquet lessons.

Background

Where I grew up and, in many ways, where I still think of as home. I always enjoy going back there; just wandering round the West End and town centre. Lunch at Tiso's is a must, getting a beer in a West End pub can't be missed and looking at the city from any of the hills to the North always feels good.

For folk that weren't lucky enough to grow up there, there's a lot to look at as well ;o) In recent years, they've made the most of the many Mackintosh assets that are scattered across the city. Have a wander up to the Art College in Garnet Hill (above Sauchiehall Street). Mackintosh designed the whole thing, so you'll recognise the iconic styles from the moment you enter to the moment you leave. If you sneak up to the top floor, there's a great view of the city.

The town centre retains a lot of mainly Victorian aspects. There are some great old boozers and lots of the stores have retained the original features. This place was the music and drama academy until recent years.
