Watermouth, Summer 2014

During August we headed down to the North Devon coast and pitched up at Watermouth Bay just east of Ilfracombe. We were trying out our new awning and it worked really well. We got a tie down strap, which was just as well as the wind was crazy all week.

Another summer, another beach, another great day for Patrick & Bryher down in the sand and surf :) Meanwhile I got to go fly a kite over Ilfracombe.

Just a short walk from the camp site, was this quirky little place called Watermouth Castle. A Victorian folly built as a wedding gift for a young bride, it had been turned over the past 40 years by one family into a slightly odd, but great fun venue.

Damien Hirst has a connection with this place. This little beauty is his understated donation to the town council. He calls it Verity

We headed out for a day trip on a little fishing boat. Kids had great fun bouncing around in the surf.

On the way home, we stopped off at Lynmouth and went up the funicular to Lynton. Great views of the bay and a memorable ice-cream at the top :)
