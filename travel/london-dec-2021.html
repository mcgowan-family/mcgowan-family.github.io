London, December 2021

Despite the emergence of the new Omicron variant, we decided to cram in one last day trip before Christmas and headed down to London.

We headed down to the river and got on the new Uberboat. It really is the best way to see London.

We took the boat from St.George's Wharf in Vauxhall all the way down to Greenwich. It was a cold day and we were all very happy to get into the excellent Coach and Horses in Greenwich Market. After a great lunch, we climbed up the hill to the Royal Observatory, home of the Greenwich Meridian! Other meridians are available, but this is the original and best :) The views from up there are wonderful, especially on an atmospheric winter's day like this one. I've never seen it drop, but this is the original Greenwich time signal. Sarah was loving it all :) We took in a fabulous planetarium show, with the excellent Greg the Astronomer, before heading back down in the dusky light to Greenwich.

It was dark when we got back down into Greenwich and the Cutty Sark looked very splendid. We got back on our boat and headed back upstream, under the dazzling Tower Bridge, and got off at Embankment.

Due to Omicron, most of the theatres had closed their doors that week. We were very lucky and managed to rebook for a production at the Royal Opera House called The Wolf, the Witch, the Giant and the Fairy. We had no idea what it would be like, but as soon as the curtain went up, the quality of the production was clear to see and it just got better as it went on. the production company was called Little Bulb and we will certainly look out for them next year.

We stayed in a very handy hotel in Holborn, just round from the British Museum. So the next morning was spent there and then on into Covent Garden. We finished up with a lovely lunch at the wonderful Lamb & Flag, an old hauunt of Charles Dickens, so very fitting for the season.
