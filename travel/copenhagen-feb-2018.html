Copenhagen, February 2018

For Sarah's birthday this year, we headed of to Copenhagen - in February!!. It is listed as a great detsination for kids and Sarah's good friend Joey lives there, so it would be nice to catch up with her as well.

As you might imagine, it was Baltic! Oh yeah, and Patrick had left his jacket in the departure lounge at Heathrow. So we did a fair bit indoors. Copenhagen now hosts Europes largest aquarium, The Blue Planet. It is absolutley fantastic and well worth a day.

We had to do the compulsory boat tour of the canals and harbours that run through Copenhagen. It's a lovely trip - although as many will have noted, the Little Mermaid is something of an anticlimax.

One of the highlights was Tivoli Gardens. We were so lucky in that Tivoli was open for the very first time this year during the February half term! It had been decked out in wintry garb. We got all-day tickets that meant the kids could just jump on and off the rides. The whole place was magical.Moss was certainly carried away by it all. Some rides were lovely period ones, whereas others were scary modern beasts. Bryher went strangely quiet at this point, 63 metres up!

Another good indoor venue is the Experimentarium. This is a bit like @Bristol, but bigger and newer. Patrick & Bryher had quite a crowd on this experiment that involved calming your brain signals to win the day. There were plenty of hands-on experiments too!

Copenhagen itself is a centre of broad boulevards and historic palaces and churches. There are plenty of museums, if that's your thing, and the National Museum of Denmark has lots of stuff on those famous Vikings!

We finished as we had started, having magical fun inTivoli, and looking forward to the next time we would be back..
