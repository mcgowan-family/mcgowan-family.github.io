Devon Spring 2012

It was time to take the plunge and go on holiday with the outlaws. We found what turned out to be this perfect little cottage down in the hamlet of Bucks Mills.

Day one was spent down on the beach getting the hang of running away from waves and chucking sand!

The weather was generally great, but on day 2, the heavens opened. Fortunately there was an activity park nearby where, among other things, Patrick got some good tips on archery. We could come to regret that.

Clovelly is a lovelly little village that tumbles down the cliff into the sea. Like Bucks Mills, there's a nice waterfall and a lovely beach to muck about on. Grandma was happy with the little lanes down on the sea front, but she jumped in the Land Rover for the climb back up the hill.

There's a donkey ride up at the top of the village and Bryher was very serious about getting kitted out. They were both great at hanging on and looked after their steeds - Jasper and Jakie.

And so we spent the week. Going fishing here and there, mucking about the cottage, a really lovely trip to Lundy (where we'll return), cream teas in Appledore and mucking about on the beach at Westward Ho!

And when it drew to a close on a lovely sunny day in April, we just had time for one more walk along the cliffs at Clovelly.
