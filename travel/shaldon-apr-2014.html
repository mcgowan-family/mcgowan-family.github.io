Shaldon, Spring 2014

Sarah booked a week for us in Devon in April. We hadn't heard of Shaldon before, but it turned out to be the loveliest little place down on the South Devon coast.

The kids took their bikes, and for the first time, we could all go for a walk at a normal pace.

It was Easter, and there was lots of egg hunt fun.This was us making Easter eggs. Bryher and Patrick seem to enjoy striking a moody pose. Then it was girls together.

We bumped into little Henry Haddock and his Mum & Dad. Turns out they're Shaldon regulars.

They have the oldest passenger ferries in England in Shaldon. And we got to ride them nearly every day.

We were just down the rail line from the section that was washed out at Dawlish.

We parked up the van and then didn't drive it for a week. Spent the whole time going for lovely walks, playing on the beach, having picnics on the beach and just basking in the warm glow of some lovely spring sunshine.

To round it off, we stopped off at a theme park on the way home. It was torture really, but the kids loved it :)
