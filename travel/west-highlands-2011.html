West Highlands 2011

Memories of our trip to Normandy and Brittany had faded sufficiently to allow us to think that going away in the van for a couple of weeks would be good fun.

We stopped off with Dave & Jules in Wetherby, then headed for Glasgow. A hectic couple of days followed where we caught up with everyone, then it was back into the van and off to the Western Highlands.

Our first stop was on the shore of Loch Linnhe, just North of the Connel Bridge at North Ledaig. It was a beautiful first pitch and what a treat to be able ot roll out of the van onto the beach. Dinner was fish & chips that night, and everything seemed to rhyme with our own childhood days as Sarah and Patrick headed off to get the fish suppers.

From Oban, we caught the CalMac to Mull. We hadn't realised how busy it would be and only just managed to squeeze the van on! We were now on holiday proper and the atmosphere was expectant on board. Mull looked great from the boat, with Castle Dornie the first thing you see.

I had Calgary Bay in mind as somewhere we might head to. But the road there, was a delight in itself. This is looking over the edge of the Eas For, where Patrick practiced his fishing just upstream of the falls.

When we finally made it to Calgary Bay, we found that the formal site was more of an encampment, that took the edge of what was a truly remarkable landscape. But we opted to stay just off the beach and try our first bit of wild camping. Sarah wasn't too sure, but the views overcame her caution :)

There's no such thing as the wrong weather, just the wrong clothing. Fortunately Patrick and Bryher had the right clothing :) We walked up to the arts centre come cafe and had a lovely lunch. The path back is littered with nice sculptures, some of which double as toddler dens. Bryher was a little wood elf and the setting couldn't have been better.

From Calgary Bay, we headed round to Tobermory. Even a bit of a beach, is still a beach and Patrick and Bryher had great fun.

I decided it was time for a treat, so we got the best room in the best hotel in town - The Western Isles. Bryher was in heaven. Her Mum thought it was pretty good too :)

Tobermory had been kind to us, but it was time to head on. We took the little ferry over to Arnamurchan and from there, headed up through Moidart to Mallaig. The weather turned nasty for a day and we spent the night in a B&B in Mallaig. That's where we met Captain Tommy of the CalMac and he told us about Camusdarach beach.

I had heard of Camusdarach before. It was the beach where Local Hero was filmed, but I'd never been there. What an amazing place. We spent all day there and Patrick and Bryher were in their element.

It was a sad farewell to the Highlands and as we drove past Glen Finnan and stopped off at the Falls of Falloch, we were sure we'd be back.
