Barmouth, August 2020

We decided to get away for a break and Sarah booked a lovely little cottage in the oldest part of Barmouth known as the Rock. Moss was so excited he couldn't go to bed and fell asleep on the landing. The journey up was easy and full of new sights, like this old toll bridge at Penmaenpool.

We were lucky with the weather and spent much of the week on beaches. Barmouth has a bit of a Glastonbury feel to it. But with Waltzers! Moss wasn't convinced about that one (!) so sat out the next ride. This was more his thing :)

Back on the beach and Bryher was striking one of her theme poses. There was plenty of lovely Sea Holly everywhere.

One of the things that attracted us to north Wales was Harlech Castle, which Patrick had studied in school. Sadly we could only admire it from outside due to Covid, but that was impressive enough and we will surely be back.

We spent the only damp day, doing a tour of Snowdonia. This is the Pont-y-Pair bridge over the Conwy in Betws-y-Coed. We got to relive early childhood memories for Sarah on the Blaennau Ffestiniog Railway. It was a lovely day and a great way to see the countryside.

Meanwhile, back at the beach, Moss and Bryher went crabbing. We even convinced the kids to climb up the hill behind the cottage to Dinas Oleu, the very first National Trust property donated by Mrs Fanny Talbot in 1895. Followed by a bit more crabbing for Patrick!

We spent one long day riding on boats and trains across the estuary to Fairbourne. First you cross the Mawddach estuary on the old wooden rail bridge. Then take the little steam train from Fairbourne (the first place in the UK that will be sacrificed to rising sea levels) back up to the spit of shingle directly across from Barmouth. Before taking a little boat back across the Mawddach into Barmouth.

Patrick and me finally got to go fishing!

We rounded off the trip by stopping off at Mark and Vic's in Coed Devonald by Eglwyswrw in Pembrokeshire.
