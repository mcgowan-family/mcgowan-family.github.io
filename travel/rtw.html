Round the World in 70 Days

Or is it 71? Whatever.

When I returned from the US to live in the UK again in 2003, I did it as a 'round the world trip. The RTW started from Cheltenham on 20 January. We flew out of Heathrow back to Dulles. Starting from London is a prerequisite of the RTW tix that agents sell - it's a hassle for the traveller.

I had a couple of weeks work to finish off in DC, then flew out from BWI on 2 February, transitted via LAX and arrived in the Cook Islands on 3 February.

A week in the Cooks is to be recommended. Two weeks would be better still. For more details, check out the Cooks page.

From the Cooks, I headed to North Island, New Zealand. A week and a half was far too little time, but we did manage to catch up with Carl, which was just right.

Across the Cook Strait, the South Island is quite a different place. Alpine in the west and pastoral in the east, with all sorts of adventure waiting in the south.

Melbourne was the port of arrival in Australia and one of the highlights of Victoria and South Australia. The Great Ocean Road and the wine regions north of there took up the rest of the time.

A lot of folk claim that the Top End is the real Australia. Kakadu and friendly folk were the highlights.

Queensland was fabulous. The Atherton Tablelands were cool and green and diving on the Barrier Reef was an unexpected highlight.

Sydney lived up to expectations with all the sights of the harbour. A useful tip is to arrange accommodation in the airport arrivals area - cheaper than the net.

I had been warned that Singapore would be sterile and overly policed. Maybe because of all the warnings, I found it a pleasant surprise. Certainly less culture-shock than the rest of South-East Asia, but worth a visit.

And finally, back to the UK.
