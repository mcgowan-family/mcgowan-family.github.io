Ecrins 06

Headed over to France with Susan and Crispin and down to the Alps near Bourg d'Oisans. Main purpose of the trip was to take part in the Etape du Tour.

We stayed in Allamont, which is about 70 km east of Grenoble and just round from Bourg d'Oisans. It's a great area, as your on the edge of the Parc National des Ecrins with fabulous hiking, climbing, biking and paddling. There are a few downhill resorts within a few miles (including Les Deux Alpes and Alpe d'Huez) and many of Le Tour's most famous climbs are in this region (or within a stupidly long ride of it!.

After we did the Etape, we went for a wander up an alp to stretch the legs out. There was loads of wildlife around. Butterflies were everywhere and the higher you climbed, the seasons rolled back and alpine flowers appeared. There were fabulous views in every direction.

Susan wasn't convinced she'd make the climb, so we were all very pleased when we reached the Refuge de Mozelle and could settle in for a fabulous lunch. Pretty much anything is fabulous after a climb like that, but it really was quite good!

There was a local sheep herd in the area. Never seen so much Merino in one place!! And a bunch of very timid sheep dogs, but one of them just couldn't get enough attention. I think I've finaly got all the slobber out of the camera lens now.

I reckon I might well be back in Bourg :o)
