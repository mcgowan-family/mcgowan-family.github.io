' Singapore

Singapore 2003

We arrived in Singapore on 27 March, 2003

Like Sydney, picking up accommodation at the airport is simple and pretty reasonable. Having walked around the city, the Perak Lodge in Little India and the Inn on Temple Street in Chinatown both looked good if you prefer character to international bland.

Having watched Adaptation (another good movie) we had an unlikely interest in orchids. It so happens that Singapore has the world's greatest collection at its Botanic Gardens, so we checked them out.

There are plenty of great places to eat in Singapore. You can go to the nicely restored Chijmes (used to be a Catholic mission school) which has a whole bunch of restaurants, or head along to one of the local places like Lau Pa Sat market for satay.

You may choose to have a swift one before dining, in which case I can recommend the New Asia bar at the top of the Stamford Hotel. Get there in time to see the city in daylight, then just watch the lights of the city come on while you wait. It's a tourist 'must see', but Raffles is also a very nice hotel and they're relaxed about you just wandering round before retiring to the Long Bar for a Gin Sling.

A wide range of cultures mingle in Singapore and one of the best places to see this is in Little India. Wandering round the streets and markets for a couple of hours you come across amazing mosques, wonderful Hindi temples and the festival of Ching Ming (festival for the dead) being celebrated by burning fake bank notes and other offerings. The locals were all very welcoming and happy to explain what was going on.

Little India is a lot less Disneyfied than Chinatown, but even there if you get off the beaten track there's plenty to see.

There's an easy walk (it needs to be in that humidity) that you can do around the colonial district. Check out the Raffles statue, the Merlion and the techy-looking concert hall.

The stay ended with SARS breaking out across South East Asia, England won the Hong Kong 7s and then the Grand Slam; surely the end of the world is nigh. But lo, Scotland top their group for Euro 96, so life goes on.

Joke of the week comes with thanx to Yan.

"You know the world is going crazy when the best rapper is a white guy, the best golfer is a black guy, France is accusing the US of arrogance and Germany doesn't want to go to war."
