A Summer break in Paris and Eurodisney!! July 2024

I never had Disneyland on my bucket list, but it turms out the magic is for everyone :)

We caught the ferry over to Calais and headed down the Autoroute des Anglais. We got to the Airbnb late i eth evening, but happily there were spome lovely restaurants nearby, including this Lebanese one. Sarah had found a really nice apartment in the district known as Val d'Europe, just one stop west along the RER A from Disneyland

We spent a lovely first day wandering round Paris. This is Moss, taking some time out by the side of the Seine. We wandered north from the river, stopping off at Les Halles for a drink.

I had always wanted to go back to the Restaurant Chartier, to let the kids see some real Paris. It was a place i went for lunch when i travelled through Paris as a student. Our route north took us right past it, so we stopped in :) It was really wonderful to share the memory, but I won't order pig's trotters again :)

We wandered all the way up to Sacre Couer and then made our way back down to the river and along to the Eiffel Tower. Our visit coincided with the Olympics, which meant the City of Lights was even more decorated than normal.

The next day was a early start before heading to The Magical Kingdom!.

I wnjoyed Eurodisney much more than I expected. There really was something for everyone. It was another very long day, but everyone coped well with it and had a lot of fun. Moss even managed to get some air!

The day finsihed with an amazing light show over the Castle. We spent a couple of days at the park before making our way back home. Tired asnd happy.
