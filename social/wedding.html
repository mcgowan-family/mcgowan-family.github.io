The 22 of August was already famous, as the day that Richard III was killed at the Battle of Bosworth, thereby ending the War of the Roses. On the very same day, almost 300 years later, George III declared the American Colonies to be in a state of open rebellion; a condition from which they have never properly recovered. Almost 250 years were to pass before this day gained its next auspicious landmark, when Paul and Sarah became Mr & Mrs McGowan.

The day had started with me at Steve and Jas's getting my act together. I'm sure it was like this for George III as well. Some minor tweaks to the outfit, a finishing touch from Jas and we were ready to go.

Sarah had set off from Clare Mews in her gorgeous dress and met up with some unexpected guests in Cirencester.

The registry office in Cirencester seemed a good place to make our entrance. As we made our vows, and exchanged rings, it all got nicely emotional, but we made it through to kissing the bride. Apparently I didn't lean forwards enough!
