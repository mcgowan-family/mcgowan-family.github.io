Rob and Shell's Wedding, 30th July, Duntreath

This was to be the second great wedding of the year. This time, we ewre all going up - and Patrick was getting a kilt!!! As you can tell, he's very excited. Bryher was going to be a bridesmaid, so all in all, everyone was looking pretty good.

Our plans took a knock on the way up when we had a complete power failure on the Volvo just as we passed through the Lakes. Four hours on the side of the M6. Fortunately the weather was good and everyone kept their sense of humour.

We had rented a lovely wee bothy up by Gartmore, which was quite close to teh wedding venue at Duntreath by Blanefield. Patrick and Bryher had the nicest wee loft room.

It was the day of the wedding and, in a fitting way, the Gale were out in force.

We had dropped Bryher off with the rest of the girls earlier in the morning to get ready. She had had a whale of a time and kept everyone amused. With great composure, she led the bride and the other maids down towards the site of the ceremony and then the main character of the day arrived!

It was the loveliest ceremony, held in an old folly in the garden of Duntreath Castle. The weather was very kind and the sun shone on Rob & Shell. They had set up a marquee in the grounds which looked lovely.

Then there were all the photos, and of course Bryher had to get in them. We wandered off round the grounds, round the pond and found the Blane roaring along behind the boundary fence. there was plenty to explore and John and Hugo went off for a wander as well.

Rob and Shell came back for a great reception from the crowd. It was time to get the party started, and Ash sang a lovely lament. The dancing kicked off. Not a great draw for wee boys, so Patrick went and found a wall to climb on. But he did seem to make a special friend!

As the evening wore on, the dancing got wilder, but for those getting long in the tooth, it was time for bed.

We had a lovely next day, exploring around Gartmore and catching up with cousins. then it was back down the road to Cheltenham. A lovely wedding and a lovely weekend, that none of us will forget.
