Tour de France 07

The Tour (or as all the blurb kept calling it the Turn) came to London over the weekend of 7/8 July. Lots of folk from Cheltenham went down to see the Prologue on the Saturday. I was lucky enough to get some hospitality from CSC thanks to Neil Arthur and Richard Morgan of CSC. It was a great day.

While the main Chelt group ended up in Hyde Park, me and Neil were in Horse Gaurds Parade. We got close up to the start line. This is Thomas Voeckler signing on and setting off. Fred Rodriguez from Predictor-Lotto looks very determined.

Some of the CSC team took us round to the team bus and showed us the bikes. It's just as well I'm not a geek, or I might have gone into too much detail. Anyway, keeping it high level, check out this hub!!! Everything had little, golf ball-like, dimples on it to reduce drag. $1500 for the wheels.

While we were there, Stuart O'Grady came back in with his back all ripped up from the crash. Very exciting. Frank Schleck (who came third in Liege-Bastogne-Liege) was getting his bike ready while we were there. All the other teams were there, this is Axel Merckx warming up with the other T Mobile guys.

We headed over to the finishing straight and I got some good pic's of riders coming into the home straight. This is Heinrich Haussler for Gerolsteiner. There had been loads of stuff about Bjarne Riis in the build up. He had fess'd to using EPO in his riding days and, as a result, had been moved to one side for this years tour. But he was still there in the background. Anyone know who he's talking to?

So things started to hot up and the big names started rolling in:

  • Iban Mayo
  • Andreas Kloden

    • So could one of the Brits make it the dream start to the Tour?

    They all did well, cheered on by the crowd. Here's David Miller coming home. And then Bradley Wiggins.

    Vino was really going for it.

    But it was the reigning world road time trial champion and CSC team member Fabian Cancellara who put an amazing 13 second on everyone else, to finish the 8km in 8 minutes and 50 seconds. It turns out that was my predicted time, As ever, no money on it.

    What a great day! The rest of the guys had had a good time as well and Sarah came and found me on Victoria Street and headed back to Cheltenham. How long will we need to wait till it comes back???
