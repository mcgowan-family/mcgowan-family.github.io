Gran Fondo Campagnolo, June 2007

This year's big Euro epic was to be the Gran Fondo Campagnolo over the weekend of 16/17 June.

Steve P had been there and done it many times, so we had a head start on all the logistics of flights/car hire/accommodation. We stayed in the great little town of Primolano which the Fondo would pass through later that weekend. Everyone agreed that the accommodation was great.

The route was a massive 217km and 15 000 vertical feet. the Fondo web site has details, but you can see it clearly on this map as it leaves Feltre and swings out through Primolano, over the Passo Mangene, along the Travignolo Valley, over the Passo di Rollo, down into San Martino di Castrozza and then finally over the Passo Croce d'Aune, before finishing back in Feltre.

As ever, there was a fair bit of faffing, but pretty soon the big day was on us.

We all did well, and those not riding were out on the course at various points to cheer us on.

After a bit of recovery time in the sun, we did some exploring in the valley. We got to make some new friends in Belluno.

The countryside around Primolano has lots of recent interesting history. This place guarded the gateway between the one of the Italian principalities and the Austro-Hungarian empire.

We were only there for a weekend, but it really did seem much longer. Finally it was time to go, so we packed up the bikes, got in one last hike in the foothills of the Dolomites and headed home.

Great trip and thanks to Steve for doing a lot of the organisation.
