Northern Corries, Cairngorm, Feb 06

Headed up to northern corries of Cairngorm with Raph. I'd tried to get ice climbing up here a couple of times before, but conditions always defeated us. We were better prepared than ever (i.e. we had axes) but again conditions seemed to be against us.

We headed up into Corrie an t-Sneachda on the first day and found a couple of the lowest graded routes with some ice. There was a party of 4 roped up in Alladin's Couloir (1 *), so we thought we'd go up and watch them. Having been told there was nothinng going, we had only taken an axe each, but we had a look and decided to work our way up the ice by using the rock on the right of the first pitch. It turned into a nice little first climb for both of us.

Day 2 saw conditions improve a little. There was still little ice, but some more snow had come down. We decided to go to Sneachda again with the plan of doing Red Gully (2 **). Another party was gearing up for that, so we switched to The Runnel (2 *) which is the central and most direct line of the Trident Gullies. It was a clear step up from the first climb in terms of steepness. The top pitch was a chimney and I was glad to clear the top in the very thin conditions (this is Raph topping out). The availability of gear suited the minimalist approach we had adopted, although Raph did have a few words to say about a certain 'nubbin' placement.

Day 3, and we decided to up the stakes again. Headed across to Hell's Lum Crag and did the Lum (2/3 ***). It feels very remote when you've hiked across the top of the plateau to get there. It was a wild day on top and no one else was making the trek, so we had the crag to ourselves. Climbed up to the bottom of the first pitch to gear up (Lesson: gear up before climbing!!). Again there was more snow, but not a lot of ice. The first pitch involved some back stepping to gain ground. Second pitch was steep snow. Third pitch was a nice ice wall on the left of the waterfall and final pitch was steep snow. It felt very good to top out :o)

On day 4 we did some hill walking above the Lairig Ghru. Fabulous conditions and we even managed a steep little snow descent back down into the pass after summiting on Sron na Lairig.

It was a fabulous week's climbing and it felt great to finally get some ice in. Walking off Cairngorm , there was a clear sense that we'd be back.
